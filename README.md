# Migration-Labour-Market
This research project examines the labour market outcomes of return migrants, focusing on wages, skill utilization, formal employment, and entrepreneurship. Using primary survey data from 300 return migrants, the study investigates how migration-acquired skills and socio-economic characteristics influence economic reintegration after return. Through descriptive statistics and econometric techniques such as OLS and logit models, the findings reveal that migration significantly contributes to human capital formation by improving skills, wages, and access to formal employment. The study also highlights an important gap between skill acquisition and labour market absorption, where many return migrants struggle to fully utilize the competencies gained abroad. Interestingly, practical skills are found to be more influential than formal education in determining labour market success, while entrepreneurship is shaped more by socio-economic factors such as age and financial capacity. By exploring the challenges of skill mismatch, institutional support, and labour market integration, this research contributes to broader discussions in migration, labour economics, and development policy, while also offering policy recommendations to strengthen employment opportunities and improve the reintegration process for return migrants.
